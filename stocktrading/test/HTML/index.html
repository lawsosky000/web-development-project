A discount broker, basic computer, and a three year old phone provide me with all the necessary infrastructure to trade stocks. Stock trading is the practice of utilizing large amounts of capital to purchase significant blocks of shares in order to take advantage of small price movements. Most traders trade the stock of corporations in the New York Stock Exchange and NASDAQ markets. I largely apply technical analysis, which is the use of previous price trends and patterns to predict the movements of future stock prices. When I trade nearly all the two hundred and fifty two trading days in a year, I wake up in the morning an hour before the market opens at 9:30 ET and begin building my watchlist. I utilize a dollar gainer and dollar loser scanner to help me efficiently target the best pattern setups that coordinate with my trading plan; this is a set of personal rules most successful traders follow that function as a boss. It is not permitted within the parameters of my plan to risk more than forty dollars on a two to one risk to reward trade. When the market opens it is crucial to anticipate when a stock might move past a resistance level, resulting in a breakout. This takes patience and discipline to keep track of and observe all the movements on my watchlist. I enter on institutional volume, or after there is a volume spike. It is critical to set a stop loss and take profit on trades, adjusting them according to the situation. I have set numerous goals to guide me to become a better trader. I record all of my trades on a spreadsheet so I can learn from my mistakes and review what I did well. Having read books such as the Intelligent Investor and the Ultimate Day Trader have expanded my knowledge of stock trading, and I am currently reading High Probability Trading by Marcel Link. I also enjoy participating in weekly virtual lectures by a former stock broker, Jared Wesley, to learn new strategies and gather ideas that may benefit my personal trading.
